## Summary: The Dividend vs. Share Repurchase Decision (CFA Level II)

---

### Core Theoretical Position

Under equal taxes and equal information content, dividends and repurchases are **equivalent in shareholder wealth effect**. The real-world debate centers on *what isn't equal* between the two.

---

### Why Companies Prefer Share Repurchases Over Dividends

| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|------------|
| **Tax advantage** | Capital gains often taxed lower than dividends; shareholders can also *defer* capital gains by not participating |
| **Signaling undervaluation** | Management buys back when they believe shares are cheap — studies show positive excess returns around announcements and for up to 5 years after |
| **Flexibility** | No legal obligation to complete an announced program; no expectation of continuance (unlike regular dividends) |
| **Offset stock option dilution** | Repurchases counteract EPS dilution from employee stock option exercises |
| **Increase financial leverage** | Reduces equity base → higher debt ratios (more so when debt-financed) |
| **Shareholder optionality** | Participation is voluntary — shareholders choose whether to sell or hold |

---

### Repurchases as a Substitute for Special Dividends

- Companies use repurchases (or special dividends) to distribute **one-time, non-recurring excess cash flows**
- Both signal: *"this is extraordinary, don't expect it to continue"*
- Repurchase preferred over special dividend when **no ongoing commitment** is desired
- Some companies initiate shareholder distributions *entirely* through repurchases to avoid setting a dividend expectation

---

### EPS Increase — A Problematic Rationale

Many managers cite EPS improvement as a reason to repurchase — but this is flawed for two reasons:
1. Higher leverage from the repurchase → **required rate of return increases**, offsetting any EPS gain
2. Changing share count alone doesn't affect shareholder wealth if **total free cash flow is unchanged**

> EPS accretion ≠ value creation — don't confuse the two on the exam.

---

### Historical Patterns — Key Data Points

| Period | Observation |
|--------|------------|
| 2000–2007 (Russell 1000) | Repurchases grew at **25% CAGR** vs. 13% for dividends |
| 2008–2009 crisis | Repurchases collapsed ($680B → $223B); dividends much more resilient ($286B → $262B) |
| 2014–2015 recovery | Repurchases recovered to $650B; dividends rose to $452B (>40% of total distributions, up from ~30% in 2007) |

**Key insight**: Repurchases are **highly cyclical** — cut aggressively in downturns when cash is scarce. Dividends are stickier. The post-crisis rise in dividend share of total distributions reflects investor appetite for yield in a low-rate environment.

---

### Dividend vs. Repurchase — Decision Framework

| Factor | Favors Dividend | Favors Repurchase |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Tax environment | Equal treatment | Capital gains taxed lower |
| Commitment desired | ✅ Yes (regular dividend) | ❌ No (one-time distribution) |
| Share price perceived as undervalued | — | ✅ Yes |
| Earnings volatility | Low volatility | High volatility |
| Stock option dilution concern | — | ✅ Yes |
| Leverage target | Already optimal | Want to increase leverage |
| Investor base | Income-focused shareholders | Growth/total return investors |
| Cash flow nature | Recurring | Non-recurring / extraordinary |

### Integrated Case — Shenzhen Medical Devices (Example 20)

A useful integrating example tying together all concepts:

**Dividend initiation critique (Director Z):** Wrong — dividend initiations are *on average* associated with **higher future earnings growth**, not stagnation. SMDL still has substantial positive NPV projects and has already been distributing cash via repurchases, so the signal is less dramatic than a cold initiation.

**Stock dividend defense (Director W):** Correct — a 2% stock dividend has **zero economic effect** on shareholder wealth. More shares, proportionally lower price — pie just cut into more slices.

**Policy classification:** SMDL's proposal reflects a **stable dividend policy with a target payout adjustment model** — commits to ¥0.40/share minimum, with a long-run 25% target payout ratio, adjusted gradually as sustainable earnings grow.

### Master Comparison Table

| Feature | Regular Cash Dividend | Special Dividend | Share Repurchase |
|---------|----------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Commitment created | ✅ Strong | ❌ None | ❌ None |
| Wealth effect (equal tax/info) | Equivalent | Equivalent | Equivalent |
| Tax efficiency | Lower (if dividends taxed higher) | Lower | Higher (deferral option) |
| Cyclicality | Low (sticky) | High | High |
| EPS effect | None directly | None directly | ↑ (if cash-funded, idle cash) |
| Leverage effect | ↑ | ↑ | ↑ (more if debt-financed) |
| Investor flexibility | None | None | ✅ Optional participation |
