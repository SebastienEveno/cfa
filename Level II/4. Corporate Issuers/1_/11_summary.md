## Summary: Module 1 Wrap-Up — Analysis of Dividends & Share Repurchases (CFA Level II)

This is the official CFA closing summary. Here's a condensed, exam-focused synthesis of the entire module.

---

### The Big Picture

A company's **payout policy** = cash dividends + share repurchases. Both distribute cash to shareholders but differ in commitment, tax treatment, signaling, and flexibility.

---

### Core Concepts — Rapid Review

**Forms of Dividends**
- Cash dividends (regular or irregular) = actual wealth transfer
- Stock dividends, splits, reverse splits = **no wealth effect** — just recarving equity into different-sized pieces
- Regular cash dividends = **commitment**; all other forms do not create the same expectation

**The Three Dividend Theories**

| Theory | View | Optimal Payout |
|--------|------|---------------|
| MM Irrelevance | Dividend policy doesn't matter in perfect markets | Irrelevant |
| Bird in Hand | Dividends less risky than capital gains | Higher |
| Tax preference | Capital gains taxed lower → prefer reinvestment or buybacks | Lower |

**Homemade dividends** (MM): shareholders can replicate any payout policy themselves by buying/selling shares → policy is irrelevant in perfect markets.

---

**Signaling**
- Initiation / increase → **positive signal**
- Cut / omission → **negative signal** (usually)
- Exception: cut to reinvest credibly can be neutral or positive

**Agency Costs**
- Dividends **reduce** manager–shareholder conflict (less free cash to waste)
- Dividends **worsen** shareholder–bondholder conflict (reduce debt cushion)

---

**Six Practical Factors**

| Factor | Direction |
|--------|----------|
| More investment opportunities | ↓ Dividends |
| Higher earnings volatility | ↓ Dividend growth |
| Need for financial flexibility | ↓ Dividends |
| Tax disadvantage of dividends | ↓ Dividends |
| High flotation costs | ↓ Dividends |
| Legal/contractual restrictions | ↓ or mandated minimum |

---

**Tax Systems**

| System | Key Feature |
|--------|------------|
| Double taxation | Taxed at corporate *and* personal level |
| Imputation | Shareholder gets franking credit → taxed once at personal rate |
| Split-rate | Lower corporate tax on distributed vs. retained earnings |

---

**Payout Policies**

| Policy | Mechanism | Volatility |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Stable dividend | Based on long-run sustainable earnings; Lintner model | Low |
| Constant payout ratio | Fixed % of current earnings | High |

**Lintner formula:**
$$\Delta DPS = (\text{Expected EPS} \times \text{Target payout} - \text{Previous DPS}) \times \text{Adjustment factor}$$

---

**Share Repurchase Methods**
- Open market → most common, most flexible, no obligation
- Fixed price tender → fast, premium to market, less flexible
- Dutch auction → price range, market-discovered clearing price
- Direct negotiation → can **destroy value for remaining shareholders** if at a premium

**EPS effect of buybacks:**
- Cash-funded → EPS likely ↑ (fewer shares, same income)
- Debt-funded → EPS ↑ only if earnings yield > after-tax cost of debt

**BVPS effect:**
- Repurchase above BVPS → BVPS ↓
- Repurchase below BVPS → BVPS ↑

**Valuation equivalence:** Buyback = cash dividend of equal amount, *all else equal*. Method of distribution does not create or destroy wealth.

---

**Dividend Safety — Key Ratios**

$$\text{Earnings coverage} = \frac{\text{Net income}}{\text{Dividends}} \quad \text{(target: 1.7x–2.5x for mature firms)}$$

$$\text{FCFE} = \text{CFO} - \text{CapEx} + \text{Net borrowing}$$

$$\text{FCFE coverage} = \frac{\text{FCFE}}{\text{Dividends} + \text{Repurchases}} \quad \text{(<1.0x = unsustainable)}$$

**Early warning signs of a dividend cut:**
- Coverage ratio declining toward 1.0x
- Abnormally high dividend yield vs. history
- Borrowing to fund dividends
- Cutting capex to sustain payouts
- High leverage limiting future debt access
- Payout ratio high relative to peers

---

### One-Page Mental Model for the Exam

```
Perfect markets → MM irrelevance → homemade dividends work
        ↓ (add imperfections)
Taxes / Asymmetric info / Agency costs → policy matters

Signaling:  Initiation/increase = good | Cut/omission = bad
Agency:     Dividends ↓ overinvestment | Dividends ↑ default risk

Payout choice:
  Stable policy (Lintner) → smooth, long-run target
  Constant payout → volatile, rare

Buyback vs. Dividend:
  Equal wealth effect (in theory)
  Buybacks: more flexible, tax-advantaged, no commitment

Safety check:
  Earnings coverage > 1.7x ✅ | < 1.0x ⚠️
  FCFE coverage > 1.0x ✅ | < 1.0x ⚠️
  Very high yield = market pricing in a cut ⚠️
```
