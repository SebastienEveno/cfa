---

### What Is ESG?

**ESG** stands for **Environmental, Social, and Governance** — three categories of non-financial factors increasingly integrated into investment analysis and security valuation.

| Pillar | Examples |
|--------|---------|
| **Environmental** | Energy use, natural resource management, waste, climate risk |
| **Social** | Worker health & safety, community impact, marketing practices |
| **Governance** | Board structure, ownership, shareholder rights, transparency |

---

### Why ESG Matters for Analysts

- **Governance** has long been recognized as a driver of long-term company performance
- **Environmental and social** factors have gained prominence more recently due to:
  - Increasingly stringent regulatory environments
  - Potentially finite natural resources
  - Global trends toward energy conservation and waste reduction
  - High-profile corporate failures linked to ESG mismanagement → **direct negative impact on security prices**

### Key Takeaway

ESG analysis gives analysts a **broader risk and opportunity lens** beyond traditional financial metrics. Mismanagement of ESG factors is not just an ethical concern — it is a **material financial risk** that can move security prices.
