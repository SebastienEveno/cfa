## Summary: Equity Valuation — Module 1 Wrap-Up (CFA Level II)

A closing summary consolidating the entire module for exam use.

---

### Core Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Valuation** | Estimation of asset value based on future return variables or comparison with similar assets |
| **Intrinsic value** | Value given hypothetically complete understanding of investment characteristics |
| **Going-concern value** | Value assuming continued operations into the foreseeable future |
| **Liquidation value** | Value if company dissolved and assets sold individually |
| **Fair value** | Price between informed, uncompelled buyer and seller |

**Foundation of active investing**: Market price **can diverge** from intrinsic value (rational efficient markets formulation / Grossman-Stiglitz).

---

### Applications of Equity Valuation

- Stock selection (primary use)
- Inferring/extracting market expectations
- Evaluating corporate events
- Fairness opinions
- Evaluating business strategies
- Appraising private businesses

---

### The Five-Step Valuation Process

| Step | Key Content |
|------|------------|
| **1. Understand the business** | Industry prospects, competitive position, strategy, financial report quality, earnings quality |
| **2. Forecast performance** | Top-down (macro→industry→company) or bottom-up (company→industry→macro) |
| **3. Select valuation model** | Match to company characteristics, data quality, and valuation purpose |
| **4. Convert to valuation** | Sensitivity analysis + situational adjustments |
| **5. Apply conclusions** | Recommendations, fairness opinions, investment decisions |

---

### Valuation Models

| Type | Definition | Examples |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Absolute** | Specifies intrinsic value directly | DDM, FCFE, FCFF, Residual Income, Asset-based |
| **Relative** | Value relative to comparable assets (method of comparables) | P/E, EV/EBITDA, P/B, P/S |

**Model selection criteria**: consistent with company characteristics + data availability/quality + valuation purpose/perspective.

---

### Converting Forecasts to Valuation

| Adjustment | Direction | Context |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| **Sensitivity analysis** | N/A | Test impact of changes in key inputs |
| **Control premium** | ↑ | Acquiring controlling stake |
| **Lack of marketability discount** | ↓ | Non-publicly traded shares |
| **Illiquidity discount / blockage factor** | ↓ | Thinly traded shares or large block sales |

---

### Effective Research Report — Key Attributes

- Timely, clear, and objective
- Key assumptions clearly identified
- **Facts and opinions clearly distinguished**
- Analysis, forecasts, valuation, and recommendation internally consistent
- Sufficient information to **critique the valuation**
- Risk factors stated
- Conflicts of interest disclosed
