# Formulas: Equity Valuation — Applications and Processes (CFA Level II)

> This module is primarily conceptual. The formulas below cover mispricing decomposition, earnings quality, and valuation adjustments.

---

## 1. Mispricing Framework

$$V^E - P = (V - P) + (V^E - V)$$

| Component | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| $V^E - P$ | **Perceived mispricing** — analyst's estimated value vs. market price |
| $V - P$ | **True mispricing** — intrinsic value vs. market price (source of alpha) |
| $V^E - V$ | **Estimation error** — analyst's estimate vs. true intrinsic value |

---

## 2. Earnings Quality — Accruals

$$\text{Accruals} = NI - (CFO + CFI)$$

- Higher accruals → lower earnings quality → lower future ROA (Sloan 1996)
- $NI > CFO$ is the primary red flag for earnings manipulation

---

## 3. Accounts Receivable Quality Ratios

$$AR\ Turnover = \frac{Revenue}{Average\ AR}$$

$$Days\ Receivable = \frac{365}{AR\ Turnover}$$

- Declining AR turnover (or rising days receivable) relative to revenue growth signals potential revenue recognition issues.

---

## 4. EBITDA — Adjusted

$$EBITDA_{adjusted} = Earnings + Interest + Taxes + D\&A\ \text{(economic only)}$$

> Add back only D&A on assets with genuine economic depreciation. Amortization of improperly capitalized costs should **not** be added back.

---

## 5. Sum-of-the-Parts (SOTP) Value

$$SOTP\ Value = \sum_{i}\ Segment_i\ Value \pm Corporate\ adjustments$$

Applied when a company operates in multiple industries. If $SOTP > EV_{market}$, a conglomerate discount exists and restructuring (spin-off, divestiture) may unlock value.

---

## 6. Situational Valuation Adjustments

| Adjustment | Direction | When Applied |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Control premium** | ↑ Value | Acquiring a controlling stake |
| **Lack of marketability discount** | ↓ Value | Non-publicly traded shares |
| **Illiquidity discount / blockage factor** | ↓ Value | Thinly traded public shares or large block sales |
