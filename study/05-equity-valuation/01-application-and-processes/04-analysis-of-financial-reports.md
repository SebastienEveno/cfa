## Summary: Analysis of Financial Reports & Sources of Information (CFA Level II)

---

### Financial Report Analysis — Strategic Context

Financial ratios gain meaning when interpreted **against a company's stated strategy**:

| Strategy | Expected Financial Signature |
|----------|---------------------------|
| **Cost leadership** | Lower gross margins but lower SG&A; high revenue/employee; higher net margin through efficiency |
| **Differentiation (brand)** | Higher gross margins; higher advertising/SG&A as % of sales; premium pricing power |

**Key metric for identifying cost leaders:**
- **Profit margin** (net income/sales) → reflects cost structure + pricing power
- **Revenue per employee** → efficiency of human capital deployment
- Higher both → likely cost leader; but must separate cost advantage from pricing power

**For early-stage/new-market companies**: non-financial metrics are critical:
- Biotech: clinical trial results
- Internet: unique visitors per day, engagement metrics

### Oilfield Services Case — Industry Analysis Framework

**Demand drivers (top-down chain):**

```
Oil & gas prices
      ↓
E&P spending by oil companies
      ↓
Demand for oilfield services
      ↓
Revenue for Schlumberger, Halliburton, etc.
```

**Key supply/demand factors to research:**
- Natural gas: inventory levels, household/commercial use, power generation mix
- Crude oil: OPEC capacity, new discoveries, depletion rates, global economic growth

**Useful data sources**: IEA, EIA, API, EUROPIA, AGA

### Sources of Information

#### Company-Provided Sources

| Source | Key Content |
|--------|------------|
| **Annual filings** (10-K / 20-F) | Business description, MD&A, competitive analysis — most detailed |
| **Interim filings** (10-Q / 6-K) | Financial statements; less industry/competitive detail |
| **Earnings press releases** | Period performance summary; issued before formal filing |
| **Conference calls** | Management elaboration + analyst Q&A; transcripts/audio available online |
| **Investor relations materials** | Presentations, webcasts, analyst day materials |

#### Regulatory Filing Databases

| Jurisdiction | Source |
|-------------|--------|
| US | SEC (www.sec.gov) — 10-K, 10-Q, 20-F, 6-K |
| Canada | SEDAR (www.sedar.com) |
| UK | Companies House |
| China (Shenzhen) | Shenzhen Stock Exchange |
| Belgium | National Bank of Belgium |

#### Important Regulatory Constraint
- **Regulation FD (US)**: prohibits companies from disclosing **material non-public information** selectively to analysts without simultaneous public disclosure
- **CFA Code of Ethics**: prohibits use of **material inside information**
- General management insights based on **public information** are still permitted and valuable

#### Third-Party Sources
- Industry associations, regulatory agencies, commercial market intelligence providers

### ESG Information Sources — Auto Industry Case Study

**Why ESG matters for automakers:**
- Resource-intensive manufacturing → heavy environmental regulation
- Worker safety → OSHA compliance; costly lawsuits and work stoppages
- Labor relations → union representation; production disruption risk
- Multi-jurisdictional regulation (EPA, European Commission, etc.)

**ESG Information Sources:**

| Source | Type | Key Content |
|--------|------|------------|
| **Corporate filings / press releases** | Company-provided | Standard disclosures |
| **Sustainability reports (CSRs)** | Company-provided | Economic, environmental, social impact; no uniform standard |
| **OSHA disclosures** | Regulatory | Safety violations history |
| **EEOC litigation database** | Regulatory | Workplace discrimination issues |
| **SASB** | Not-for-profit | Industry-specific ESG standards; quantitative financial impact |
| **Carbon Disclosure Project** | Not-for-profit | Self-reported environmental data; climate/water exposure |
| **Ceres** | Not-for-profit | Sustainability research and advocacy reports |

> **Key caveat on CSRs**: No uniform standard for issuance or disclosure — comparability across companies is limited.

### Exam Tips

- **Financial ratios must be interpreted in strategic context** — a high SG&A% may indicate differentiation strategy, not inefficiency
- **Revenue per employee** = proxy for cost leadership efficiency
- **10-K/20-F** = most comprehensive source; **10-Q/6-K** = less competitive detail
- **Regulation FD**: companies cannot selectively share material non-public information with analysts → general public information discussions still permitted
- **Sustainability reports**: useful for ESG analysis but **no uniform standard** → limited comparability
- **SASB**: best source for **quantifiable, industry-specific** ESG factors with direct financial impact
- Analyst–management meetings are valuable for **general insights** but cannot cross into material non-public information territory
