## Summary: Market-Based Valuation — Introduction to Price & Enterprise Value Multiples (CFA Level II)

---

### What Are Multiples?

**Price multiples**: Ratios of stock price to a per-share fundamental (earnings, book value, sales, cash flow)

**Enterprise value multiples**: Ratios of total firm value to a company-level fundamental (EBITDA, sales, operating cash flow)

**Appeal**: Simplicity, ease of communication, widely recognized by investors and analysts.

---

### Two Methods for Using Multiples

| Method | Description | Economic Rationale |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Method of comparables** | Compare asset's multiple to benchmark (peer group, closest competitor) | **Law of one price** — identical assets should sell at the same price |
| **Method based on forecasted fundamentals** | Derive multiple from DCF model and fundamental forecasts | Multiples should reflect expected future cash flows |

> In practice, both methods are used together — fundamentals explain why comparable companies trade at different multiples.

---

### Method of Comparables — Key Concepts

**Process:**
1. Identify comparable companies (guideline companies/comps)
2. Calculate peer group multiples (use **median** to avoid outlier distortion)
3. Apply benchmark multiple to subject company's fundamental → estimate value
4. Compare estimated value to market price

**Example (Company A):**
- EPS = $1.50, Peer P/E = 22×
- Estimated fair price = $1.50 × 22 = **$33**
- Actual price = $37.50 → P/E = 25× → **relatively overvalued** vs. peer

**Critical distinction**: Relative vs. absolute valuation:
- A stock can be **relatively undervalued** vs. peers but still **absolutely overvalued** if peers themselves are overpriced
- Always maintain the distinction: **undervalued** ≠ **relatively undervalued**

---

### Method Based on Forecasted Fundamentals

**Process:**
1. Build a DCF model → estimate intrinsic value
2. Divide intrinsic value by forecasted fundamental → derive **justified multiple**
3. Compare justified multiple to actual market multiple

**Example:**
- DCF value = £10.20, Forecasted EPS = £1.20
- Justified forward P/E = £10.20/£1.20 = **8.5×**
- Compare 8.5× to actual market P/E → assess over/undervaluation

---

### Justified Price Multiple — The Key Output

**Justified multiple** = estimated fair value of the multiple, based on either:
- Comparables (peer group benchmark)
- Forecasted fundamentals (DCF-derived)

**Decision rule:**
| Comparison | Interpretation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Justified multiple **>** actual multiple | Stock may be **undervalued** |
| Justified multiple **<** actual multiple | Stock may be **overvalued** |
| Justified multiple **≈** actual multiple | Stock may be **fairly valued** |

**Example:**
- Peer median P/B = 2.2 (justified by comparables)
- DCF-derived justified P/B = 2.0 (justified by fundamentals)
- Book value per share = $23
- Comparable-based fair value = 2.2 × $23 = **$50.60**
- Fundamental-based fair value = 2.0 × $23 = **$46.00**

---

### Momentum Indicators (Brief Mention)

- Relate price or a fundamental to its **own past values** or expected values
- Focus on **time-series patterns** of returns
- **Complementary** to price/EV multiples — different type of valuation indicator
- Not a substitute for fundamental analysis

---

### Exam Tips

- **Law of one price** = economic rationale for comparables method — know this
- **Use median, not mean** for peer group multiples — avoids outlier distortion
- **Relative undervalued ≠ absolutely undervalued** — critical distinction, frequently tested
- **Justified multiple**: can be derived from comparables OR fundamentals — both are valid
- **Forward P/E**: based on **forecasted** EPS; distinguished from trailing P/E (based on historical EPS)
- **EV multiples** (EV/EBITDA, EV/Sales): used for **all capital providers** — capital structure neutral; price multiples are **equity-only**
- Both methods (comparables + fundamentals) are typically used together — fundamentals explain differences observed in comparables
