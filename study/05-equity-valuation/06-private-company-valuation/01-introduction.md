## Summary: Private Company Valuation — Introduction (CFA Level II)

---

### What Are Private Companies?

Companies whose shares are **not listed on public markets**, ranging from:
- Sole proprietorships
- Multigenerational family businesses
- Formerly public companies taken private (MBOs, LBOs)
- Large successful companies that have always remained private (Tata, IKEA, ALDI, Cargill, Bechtel)

---

### Key Differences from Public Company Valuation

| Feature | Public Companies | Private Companies |
|---------|-----------------|------------------|
| Market price | Observable | **Not available** |
| Financial statements | Audited, periodic | May be **unaudited or incomplete** |
| Ownership structure | Dispersed | **Concentrated** (management, PE investors) |
| Liquidity | High | **Illiquid** |
| Access to capital markets | Yes | Limited or none |

---

### Why Private Company Valuations Are Conducted

| Purpose | Examples |
|---------|---------|
| **Transactions** | M&A, buyouts, ownership transfers |
| **Financial/tax reporting** | Estate taxes, gift taxes, compliance |
| **Legal disputes** | Divorce settlements, shareholder disputes |

---

### Three Key Areas of Adjustment

**1. Cash Flow & Earnings Adjustments**
- Identify and correct **financial statement inconsistencies**
- Normalize earnings to provide a reliable baseline for forecasting

**2. Discount Rate Adjustments**
- CAPM has **limited applicability** to private companies (no observable beta)
- Use **expanded CAPM** or **build-up approach**:
  - Add risk premia for company size, industry, specific company risk
  - Adjust for lack of public market access

**3. Valuation Discounts and Premiums**
- **Control premium**: buyer acquiring a controlling stake pays more
- **Lack of control discount**: minority stake worth less
- **Lack of marketability discount**: illiquid shares worth less than equivalent liquid shares

---

### Three Valuation Approaches

| Approach | Method | Public Company Analog |
|---------|--------|----------------------|
| **Income approach** | Discounted cash flow (DCF) | Same as DCF for public companies |
| **Market approach** | Price multiples of comparable firms | Method of comparables |
| **Asset-based approach** | Market value of assets − liabilities | Net asset value / liquidation value |

> **Process is conceptually identical** to public company valuation — adjustments are required for the unique characteristics of private firms.

---

### Exam Tips

- **Same valuation methods**, different adjustments — this is the central theme
- **Three approaches**: income (DCF), market (multiples), asset-based
- **Three adjustment areas**: earnings normalization, discount rate, control/marketability discounts
- **Comparable public companies** are commonly used to estimate cost of capital and multiples for private firms
- Principles apply across **all sizes, stages, and ownership structures** of private companies — also applicable to real estate and infrastructure
