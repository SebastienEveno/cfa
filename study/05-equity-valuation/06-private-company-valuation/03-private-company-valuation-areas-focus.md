## Summary: Private Company Valuation Uses & Areas of Focus (CFA Level II)

---

### Three Purposes of Private Company Valuation

#### 1. Transaction-Related

| Transaction Type | Key Features |
|-----------------|-------------|
| **Venture capital (early-stage)** | Highly uncertain cash flows; informal valuations; multiple financing rounds tied to milestones |
| **Private equity (growth/buyout)** | Growth equity = minority stake, rapid growth goal; LBO = majority control, value via operational efficiency + balance sheet optimization |
| **Debt financing** | Assess ability to repay existing debt or capacity to take on new debt |
| **IPO** | Private company accessing public markets for first time; spinoff from public company; formerly public returning after restructuring |
| **Acquisitions/divestitures** | Buy/sell of company or division; valuations by both target and buyer management + investment bankers |
| **Bankruptcy** | Going concern vs. liquidation analysis; restructuring overleveraged capital structures |
| **Share-based compensation** | Stock options, restricted stock, ESOPs — have accounting and tax implications; require valuations for private companies |

#### 2. Compliance-Related

| Type | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Financial reporting** | Performance reporting for investment firms; impairment testing after acquisitions; valuing divisions of public companies |
| **Tax reporting** | Corporate restructurings, transfer pricing, property taxes; estate and gift taxation for individuals |

#### 3. Litigation-Related

| Type | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Legal proceedings** | Damages claims, lost profits, shareholder disputes, divorce proceedings |
| **Scope** | May affect public or private companies, or only individual shareholders |

> Each practice area requires **specialized skills**: Transactions → investment bankers; Compliance → accounting/tax expertise; Litigation → legal presentation skills.

---

### Three Key Areas of Focus for Private Company Valuation

| Area | Affects | Key Issue |
|------|---------|----------|
| **1. Earnings normalization / cash flow adjustments** | **Numerator** of valuation | Adjust financial statements for private company distortions to estimate normalized earnings |
| **2. Discount rate / rate of return adjustments** | **Denominator** of valuation | CAPM assumptions don't apply; must use expanded CAPM or build-up approach |
| **3. Valuation discounts or premiums** | **Final value** | Apply control premium or lack-of-control/marketability discount based on stake characteristics |

---

### Standards of Value — Context Matters

- **Fair market value** (financial/tax reporting) may differ from
- **Investment value** (acquisition — reflects buyer-specific synergies and willingness to pay a premium)

---

### Knowledge Check Answers

**Q1 (share-based payments practice area):**
→ **A. Transaction** — Share-based payments are transactions between company and employees.

**Q2 (adjustments needed for private company DCF):**
→ **C. Both cash flow and discount rate adjustments** — Private company valuation requires normalizing cash flows (numerator) AND adjusting the discount rate (denominator).

**Q3 (personal use of company assets by executive):**
→ **B. Primarily affects the numerator** — Personal use of company assets is a form of above-market executive compensation that inflates company expenses, reducing reported earnings → must be added back when normalizing cash flows (numerator adjustment).

---

### Exam Tips

- Know all **three purposes**: transaction, compliance, litigation — with specific subcategories
- **Share-based compensation** = **transaction** purpose (not compliance)
- **Tax reporting** = **compliance** purpose
- **Three adjustment areas**: numerator (earnings normalization), denominator (discount rate), final value (discounts/premiums) — know which is which
- **Personal perks to owner-managers** = cash flow adjustment (numerator), not discount rate
- **Fair market value ≠ investment value** — purpose of valuation determines the standard used
