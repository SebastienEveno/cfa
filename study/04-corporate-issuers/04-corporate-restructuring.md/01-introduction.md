### What Is Corporate Restructuring?

Corporate restructurings are **revolutionary** (as opposed to evolutionary) changes to a company's legal and accounting structure.

| Type | Description |
|------|------------|
| **Acquisitions** | One company buys another |
| **Divestitures** | Company sells a segment of its business |
| **Spin-offs** | Company separates a segment into an independent entity |

**Common features of all restructurings:**
- Attract significant **press and analyst attention**
- Associated with **increased securities trading volume** around announcements

### Key Takeaway

The analyst's role is to evaluate restructurings **independently** — assessing whether a proposed transaction creates or destroys shareholder value, rather than accepting management's stated rationale at face value.
