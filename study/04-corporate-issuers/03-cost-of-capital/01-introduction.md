### The WACC Formula

$$\boxed{WACC = w_d r_d(1-t) + w_p r_p + w_e r_e}$$

| Component | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| $w_d, w_p, w_e$ | Weights of debt, preferred equity, common equity |
| $r_d(1-t)$ | After-tax cost of debt |
| $r_p$ | Cost of preferred equity |
| $r_e$ | Cost of common equity (required return on equity) |

All weights are non-negative and **sum to 1.0**.

---

### Two Uses of WACC

| User | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Internal decision makers** | Evaluate capital investment projects (hurdle rate) |
| **Analysts & investors** | Critical input in **company valuation** |

---

### Three Core Challenges in Estimating WACC

| Challenge | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Multiple valid methods** | No single "right" way to estimate each cost of capital component |
| **Target vs. current capital structure** | Weights should reflect **long-term target**, which may differ from today's actual structure |
| **Marginal vs. effective tax rate** | The **marginal** tax rate is theoretically correct but may differ significantly from the average/effective rate reported |

---

### Key Takeaway

WACC estimation involves **judgment-laden assumptions** — different analysts applying different methods to the same company can arrive at materially different WACCs, and therefore different valuation conclusions. Module 3 explores these complexities in depth.
