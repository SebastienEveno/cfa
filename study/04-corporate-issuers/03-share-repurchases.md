## Summary: Share Repurchases (CFA Level II)

A buyback is when a company purchases its own shares using **corporate cash** — making it a true alternative to cash dividends.

### 4 Repurchase Methods

| Method | Speed | Flexibility | Key Feature |
|--------|-------|------------|-------------|
| **Open market** | Slow | ✅ Maximum | Most common globally |
| **Fixed price tender** | Fast | ❌ Low | Company offers fixed price above market |
| **Dutch auction** | Fast | ❌ Low | Company sets price range; market discovers clearing price |
| **Direct negotiation** | Variable | ❌ Low | Negotiated with major shareholder |

### Financial Statement Effects

**Balance sheet (always):**
- ↓ Assets (cash used)
- ↓ Shareholders' equity
- → **Leverage ratios increase**

### EPS Effects

Repurchases **increase EPS** when:
- Shares are repurchased at a **price below intrinsic value**
- The company has excess cash that isn't earning adequate returns

Repurchases **decrease EPS** when:
- Shares are repurchased at a **price above intrinsic value**
- The company uses debt to fund repurchases (interest expense)
