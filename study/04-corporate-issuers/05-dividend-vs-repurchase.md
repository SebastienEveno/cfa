## Summary: Dividend vs. Share Repurchase Decision (CFA Level II)

When deciding between dividends and share repurchases, companies must consider several factors beyond simple valuation equivalence.

### Key Decision Factors

**1. Tax Considerations**
- Dividend: Taxed as ordinary income (typically higher rate)
- Repurchase: Taxed as capital gains (typically lower rate)
- In tax-advantaged jurisdictions or for tax-exempt investors: Dividends may be preferred

**2. Information/Signaling Content**
- Stable dividend: Signals confidence in sustainable cash flows
- Repurchase: Signals belief that stock is undervalued
- Market typically responds positively to dividends (commitment signal)

**3. Financial Flexibility**
- Dividends: Create expectation of continuation; difficult to reduce
- Repurchases: More flexible; can be suspended without market penalty
- In uncertain business environments: Repurchases allow flexibility

**4. Investor Base**
- Income-seeking investors: Prefer dividends
- Growth-oriented investors: Prefer buybacks (defer taxation)
- Pension/endowments: Prefer dividends (tax-exempt, need income)

**5. Capital Structure**
- Over-leveraged: Consider buybacks funded by debt reduction
- Under-leveraged: Can fund repurchases with debt if accretive
- Target leverage ratio should guide payout method choice

### Summary Framework

| Situation | Preferred Method | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| Stock significantly undervalued | Repurchase | Best use of capital |
| Sustainable high cash flows | Dividend | Strong signal to market |
| Volatile earnings | Repurchase | Maintain flexibility |
| Tax-disadvantaged investors | Dividend | Better tax treatment |
| Need to reduce leverage | Repurchase + debt reduction | Addresses both issues |
