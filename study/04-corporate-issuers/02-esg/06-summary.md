## Summary: ESG Considerations in Investment Analysis — Module 2 Wrap-Up (CFA Level II)

A short closing summary. Here's the key points consolidated for exam use.

---

### Ownership Structures

| Type | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Dispersed** | Many shareholders, none with individual control |
| **Concentrated** | One shareholder or group (controlling shareholders) holds control |
| **Hybrid** | Mix of both |

**Control mechanisms that disconnect ownership from voting power:**
- **Horizontal ownership** — cross-shareholdings between companies with mutual interests
- **Vertical/pyramid ownership** — controlling interest in holding companies that control operating companies
- **Dual-class shares** — superior voting rights for one share class vs. inferior/none for another

**Types of influential owners**: banks, families, sovereign governments, institutional investors, group companies, private equity, foreign investors, managers, board directors

---

### Board Structure

| Structure | Composition |
|-----------|------------|
| **One-tier** | Single board of executive + non-executive directors |
| **Two-tier** | Supervisory board overseeing a management board |

**CEO duality** = CEO also serves as board chairperson → governance red flag

---

### ESG Integration — Key Principles

**Primary challenge**: ESG data is **voluntary, inconsistently reported, and difficult to compare**

**Materiality**: ESG issues expected to affect **operations, financial performance, or security valuation**

**Three sources of ESG information:**
1. Proprietary research (analyst's own tools/judgment)
2. ESG data providers (MSCI, Sustainalytics)
3. Not-for-profit frameworks (GRI, SASB, IIRC)

**Governance** factors tend to be **consistent** across companies; **Environmental and Social** factors are **highly industry-specific**

---

### Equity vs. Fixed Income ESG Integration

| | Equity | Fixed Income |
|--|--------|-------------|
| **Goal** | Opportunities + downside mitigation | Downside mitigation only |
| **Starting point** | Material qualitative + quantitative ESG factors | Same |

---

### One-Page Mental Model for the Exam

```
Ownership Structure
├── Dispersed → Principal-Agent problem (weak shareholders, strong managers)
├── Concentrated → Principal-Principal problem (strong controllers, weak minorities)
└── Control ≠ Ownership → dual-class shares, pyramids, cross-holdings

Board Evaluation
├── Independence ↑ = better (especially in dispersed markets)
├── CEO duality = red flag
├── Tenure >10 years = independence concern
└── Key committees must be independent (audit, comp, nomination)

ESG Integration
├── Identify material factors (industry-specific for E and S; consistent for G)
├── Adjust financials: revenues, COGS, margins, capex, impairments
├── Adjust valuation: discount rate (equity) or credit spread (debt)
└── Equity = upside + downside | Fixed income = downside only
```
